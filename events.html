  • Event 2

    Date: [First week in every semester]

    Description: Interaction with Old Students and IT Professors: A Reunion of Minds Overview The event, "Interaction with Old Students and IT Professors," aims to foster a sense of community within the department by bringing together current students with those who have successfully traversed the academic journey and have since made impactful contributions to the field of Information Technology. Agenda 1. Keynote Addresses: Esteemed IT professors, both current and retired, will deliver insightful keynote addresses, sharing their experiences, thoughts on the evolving landscape of technology, and advice for current students embarking on their own professional journeys. 2. Panel Discussions: Engage in thought-provoking panel discussions where both old students and professors will share anecdotes from their time at the department, discuss industry trends, and explore the future of technology. 3. Networking Sessions: A unique opportunity for current students to connect with successful alumni and IT professionals. Whether you're seeking career advice, mentorship, or collaboration on a project, this event provides a conducive environment for networking. 4. Alumni Showcases: Highlighting the achievements of our esteemed alumni, showcasing their career paths, innovative projects, and the impact they've made in the IT industry. 5. Interactive Workshops: Participate in hands-on workshops led by industry experts and professors, covering cutting-edge topics and technologies. How to Participate - Students: This event is a fantastic opportunity to gain insights, seek mentorship, and expand your professional network. Attendance is free for all current students. - Alumni: We invite all Computer Science Department alumni to come and share their experiences, reconnect with former classmates and professors, and contribute to the growth of our community. - Professors: Join us in fostering a sense of continuity and knowledge exchange. Your presence will greatly enrich the discussions and provide valuable guidance to current students. We look forward to creating lasting connections and fostering a spirit of collaboration between the current generation of students and the trailblazers who have paved the way before them. Let's celebrate the past, embrace the present, and shape the future together! See you there! [Dr. Livingstone MC] [PRO] Computer Science Department University of Ghana ]
